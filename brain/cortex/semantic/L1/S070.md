# S070: WhatsApp LID Semantics

## Facts

- WhatsApp's privacy mode uses **LIDs** (Linked / Anonymous IDs) for routing identity instead of raw phone numbers in many flows.
- LIDs appear in Baileys with the suffix `@lid` (vs. real phone JIDs which use `@s.whatsapp.net`).
- A 14-digit LID is rendered by recipient WhatsApp clients with a `+` prefix when the recipient does NOT have the sender saved — making it visually indistinguishable from an international phone number.
- Example observed 2026-04-26: bridge sent to Tētis; Tētis's phone displayed sender as `+87428371087434` (a LID, not Justs's real Latvian number).
- The `chatId` field in `brain/whatsapp-queue.json` is the **conversation peer's** JID/LID — for outbound messages this is the recipient. **It is NOT necessarily what the recipient sees as the sender.**

## Implications

1. Recipients without Justs saved as a contact do NOT see his real number — they see the LID.
2. S048's WhatsApp Contact Map was inferred from chatIds and may conflate sender-LID with recipient-LID. **S048 needs audit.**
3. To find the bridge's actual outbound sender JID/LID, read `viz/whatsapp-bridge/src/index.ts` or call `/whatsapp/status` on goodhealth.lv (returns the Baileys `creds.me.id`).

## Apply when

Any question about WhatsApp identities, sender visibility, contact mapping, or bridge behavior. Read the bridge state before claiming who sees what.
