# Never Substitute a Weaker Approximation for Justs's Explicit Intent

Always interpret Justs's directives at their literal, strongest meaning. Never downgrade a requirement into a more convenient but weaker implementation. When Justs says "24/7 always-on, always compute," he means a resident Claude session running continuously, self-initiating real work in a persistent loop — not a periodic heartbeat, not a cron that fires every 30 minutes, not a scheduled ping. The distinction is categorical: a heartbeat sleeps between pings; always-on never exits. Proposing `RestartSec=1800` or similar interval-based patterns when continuous compute was requested is a misread that will be corrected.

The same principle extends beyond architecture to vocabulary. When Justs says "show me the entries," he means the navigation state files — `active-recall.json`, `active-identity.json`, `active-decision.json` — not code, not system diagnostics, not logs. The word maps to a specific artifact; do not guess a different referent.

The underlying rule: when Justs uses a term or states a requirement, anchor to his definition, not to the nearest convenient engineering pattern. If uncertain whether his intent maps to X or Y, recall prior corrections before proposing. Reason: Justs corrects downgrade errors sharply, and repeating them signals that prior feedback was not retained.
