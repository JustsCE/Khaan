# R134: Interpretation-Table Structure — Sentences Over Metrics

**Rule:** When asked for an "interpretation" table of a statistical model, structure it as one row per entity (variable / item / factor) with a plain-language sentence describing what that entity means for the outcome. Do NOT produce a 3-column metric/value/jargon dump.

**Why:** Justs rejected my first interpretation table (rows = Rādītājs/Vērtība/Interpretācija with "Hosmera-Lemešova χ²", "McFaddena pseido-R²", "Nagelkerkes R²", "c-statistika (ROC AUC)" — statistical jargon dumped into a table). Corrections:
- "no statistical definitions (Hosmera??? McFaddena??)"
- "ensure write with words/sentences - so 1 column for the variables in the regression and another for simple human explanation"
- "then 1 row summarizing the n, VIF, R2, p, other"

**Correct format:** Two columns — Mainīgais | Skaidrojums. One row per variable, with a plain-language sentence in the Skaidrojums column. Plus one final italicized row "Statistiskās piezīmes" combining n, R², AUC, VIF, atbilstības p into a single sentence.

**Wrong format:** Three columns — Rādītājs | Vērtība | Interpretācija — where rows are statistical metrics (Hosmera-Lemešova χ², McFaddena pseido-R², Nagelkerkes R², c-statistika). Statistician names dumped into table.

**How to apply:**
1. When stakeholder is non-statistician (medical collaborator, business user): name things in plain Latvian/English.
2. One diagnostic summary row at the bottom combines n, R², AUC, VIF, atbilstības p — italicized, not jargon-named.
3. Strip statistician names (Hosmer, Lemeshow, McFadden, Nagelkerke) — use just "R²", "AUC", "atbilstības p".
4. Variable-to-sentence mapping > metric-to-value mapping.
