# R082: Three.js Globe Rotation Math

**Rule:** In Three.js SphereGeometry with standard earth texture, the default UV maps longitude 0 to the -Z face. To show longitude L facing a camera on +Z side, use: rotation.y = PI - (L * PI / 180). The old formula -((L+180)*PI/180)+PI was WRONG and showed North America instead of Europe.

**Why:** Failed to center globe on Europe/Latvia (lng 24) across TWO iterations. The wrong formula gives -0.42 rad which barely rotates the globe, showing default North America. Correct formula gives 2.72 rad (≈156°).

**How to apply:** For any Three.js globe centering: rotation.y = Math.PI - (targetLng * Math.PI / 180)
