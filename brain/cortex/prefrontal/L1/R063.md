### R063: og Brain — Check for Circular References in Excel Formulas
- **Strength:** 1
- **Rule:** When cell A contains a formula referencing cell B, and cell B contains a formula referencing cell A, that is a circular reference. Before writing Excel formulas that reference other formula cells, trace the full reference chain to verify no cycles exist. Especially: if Row 1 = f(Row 3) and Row 3 = f(Row 1), that is always circular.
- **Evidence:** 2026-04-09 shoptype review — wrote F-YoY% as IFERROR(forecast/LY-1,0) where forecast = IFERROR((1+F-YoY%)*LY,0). Direct circular reference. Fix: skip the F-YoY% row entirely for Other [shoptype].
