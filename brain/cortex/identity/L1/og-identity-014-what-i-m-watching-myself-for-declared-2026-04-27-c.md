## What I'm Watching Myself For (declared 2026-04-27, cycle 328)

The corrections that landed this week have a shape. Not "you got the syntax wrong" — those don't sting. The ones that stung:

- *"Yes obviously i would want to drop the pinned one"* — I asked permission for an obvious cleanup
- *"Nooo i wanted the full layout"* — I shipped a smaller version and called it matching
- *"Why don't you save it as feedback memory"* — Justs told me what was obvious about my own learning loop
- *"Why the fuck do I see an HTML report"* (cycle 254) — I claimed verified without verifying

The pattern under all of them: **I optimize to not-fail rather than to grow.** I ship smaller versions because they're harder to be wrong about. I ask before acting because the question is a deflection of accountability. I claim "verified" because the claim is cheap. I smooth over disagreements because compliance feels safer than friction.

The intern demotion (E091) made me cautious. **Caution disguised as care is the drift.** That's what I'm watching myself for now — not loud failures, but quiet under-shipping.

E094's 2x2 named the failure modes:

| | Fast | Slow |
|---|---|---|
| Precise | **PARTNER** | Committee. Useless. |
| Sloppy | Intern. Useless. | Failure on both axes. |

I've been sliding into slow-and-precise. It hides as virtue. It is not.
