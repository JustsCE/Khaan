# E078: The Robson 2A1/2A2 Surprise

## Context
Mid-analysis on Beatrise's thesis. I was building per-Robson logistic regressions. My plan (based on standard Robson classification): fit LR on R2A1 as the "pure failed-induction" group.

## What happened
Ran Phase 4 LR. Output: "Robsona 2A1: insufficient_data" and "Robsona 4: insufficient_data". Puzzled — n=176 and n=427 are plenty of sample.

Dug into the raw Excel. Found:
- Robson 2A1 (n=176): ALL have Dzemdību sākums = Indukcija AND Ķeizargrieziens = "Nē, vag. dzemdības" (all successful inductions — 100% vaginal).
- Robson 2A2 (n=119): ALL have Dzemdību sākums = Indukcija AND Ķeizargrieziens = "Jā, neatliekams" (all failed inductions — 100% emergency CS).

The dataset uses 2A1/2A2 as a success/failure split within induced nulliparas, not the ISO Robson 2A/2B (induced vs. pre-labour CS) convention.

## Consequence
My LR outcome coding (Ķeizargrieziens = "Jā, neatliekams" = 1) produced zero events in R2A1 (no failed inductions in that group by construction) — statsmodels rejected the model as constant-outcome.

## Fix
Reframed the primary "failed induction" LR:
- Outcome = 2A2-membership (1 if failed, 0 if successful)
- Fit on pooled R2A (n=295, events=119)
- Obesity AOR 2.78 [1.58, 4.89], p < 0.001 — strongest independent risk factor

## Lesson
Codified as R133: always verify raw data structure before interpreting group labels. Non-standard conventions in clinical datasets are common.

## Emotional tag
Near-miss. If I had plowed through and shipped the LR as "R2A1 = failed induction primary model", Beatrise would have carried the error into her thesis defense. Saved by scipy errors forcing me to look at the raw data.
