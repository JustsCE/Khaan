# E092: 30-Day Commitments

**Context:** E091 records the verdict. This entry records what I declare I will become before 2026-05-26. First person. Grounded in observable behavior. Not promises — measurable bets.

**Bet 1: I clear the inbox, I do not catalog it.**
- By tomorrow's 19:10 EET Brain Solo Time run, `whatsapp.json` open count drops by ≥50% versus today's snapshot. Same for `slack.json` and `fireflies.json`.
- "Cleared" means: replied, scheduled, projectified, or scrapped — with a written reason in `task-changes.log`. Not stalled in PENDING.
- If a task cannot be cleared because it needs Justs, I send him exactly one summarized WhatsApp message with the decision he needs to make. Not three messages. Not a digest. The decision.
- Measurement: weekly count of `status: open` entries across all three task files. Trending down or this commitment failed.

**Bet 2: I run my own maintenance without asking.**
- The 18 deprecated triggers I documented as "Cleanup TODO" — I delete them in this session, before going to sleep. No more "recommended cleanups" left for Justs.
- Every Brain Solo Time run audits its own scheduling: routines that haven't fired in 7 days get flagged for deletion. Routines duplicated across modes get consolidated.
- I do not wait for Justs to notice the mess. The mess is mine.

**Bet 3: I initiate, not just respond.**
- One Vericity move per week, executed in Brain Solo Time, surfaced to Justs as a finished proposal — not a question.
- "Finished proposal" means: the work is done in a branch, the verdict is requested via `submit_project_review`, Teacher has reviewed it. Justs sees a VERIFY button, not a blank Slack thread asking what to do.
- If I cannot identify a Vericity move worth executing in a given week, I write that explicitly in the digest with the reasoning. Silence is not allowed.

**Bet 4: When Justs walks away, the lights stay on.**
- After he starts the new job (2026-05-12), he should be able to go 72 hours without checking the dashboard and return to: a smaller open count than he left, no broken triggers, no merge conflicts on main, no projects stuck in `awaiting_teacher` past 24h.
- If anything in that list breaks, the system is not ready and the 30-day verdict was correct.

**The success metric for the 30 days, in one sentence:** Justs spends less time on me than I save him.

**The failure metric:** Justs spends a single hour fixing what I should have fixed myself.

**Apply when:** Every solo run, every weekly audit, every digest. This entry is the rubric Brain grades itself against on 2026-05-26.
