# og Brain — Logistic Regression Methodology — Beatrise Thesis

## Scoping rule (CRITICAL)
**Logistic regression is fit PER ROBSON GROUP, not pooled.** There is NO "total" regression.

Justs (2026-04-23): *"I don't need a 'total' regression - makes no sense. The Regression needs to be in scope of the Robson groups."*

Rationale: the thesis framework is Robson-stratified. A pooled model would confound fundamentally different obstetric populations (nulliparous spontaneous vs. induced vs. previous CS vs. breech). Each Robson group is clinically its own universe.

Methodology sentence to use:
> "Loģistiskās regresijas modeļi tika veidoti **atsevišķi katrai Robsona grupai** ar pietiekamu paraugu."

Only fit models in groups where sample size permits (rule of thumb: ≥10 events per predictor).

## Variable-selection requirement
Section 3.6 paragraph 2 MUST explicitly:
1. **List included predictors + reason per variable** (signal from chi²/MW OR established clinical confounder)
2. **List excluded predictors + reason** (multicollinearity, outcome-adjacent, clinically distant)
3. **Report VIF check** confirming no multicollinearity after selection

Selection must be **rationalised from chi²/MW findings** — not "throw everything in".

## Standard candidate predictors (to be refined by chi²/MW results per group)
- Gestācijas diabēts
- Hipertensija
- Indukcija (where outcome ≠ failed induction itself)
- Multipara
- Vecums (as continuous)

## Standard exclusions + reasons
- **Preeklampsija** — collinear with Hipertensija
- **Rēta uz dzemdes** — collinear with Multipara / previous CS (captured via Robson group)
- **Augļa svars** — outcome-adjacent (determined at delivery, not pre-labour)
- **Izglītība / Profesija / Attiecības / Smēķēšana** — statistically significant in bivariate tests but clinically distant; not mechanistic for the outcome

Final included set may differ per Robson group based on chi²/MW signal within that group.
