# og Brain — PR111: Writing Statistics Thesis Sections (Latvian Academic)

**Baseline procedural for /action-train thesis-stats-writing.** Consolidates everything Brain has learned across 3 source PDFs + Robson report prior art. Gets updated after each calibration round.

## Cue
Write a section of a Latvian-language statistics thesis — Methods, Results, Discussion, or Conclusions. Current primary use case: Beatrise's diploma thesis built on JustsCE/stats Robson notebook.

## Routine

### Step 1 — Identify section type and apply its skeleton
Latvian medical/statistics theses follow a 6-section template:
- **3. MATERIĀLI UN METODES** — methods boilerplate (see Step 2)
- **4. REZULTĀTI** — results (see Step 3)
- **5. DISKUSIJA** — discussion (see Step 4)
- **6. SECINĀJUMI** — conclusions (see Step 5)

### Step 2 — Methods section boilerplate ([[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[H-627 (resolved) (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)], S063)
Fixed sequence, single paragraph each:
1. Study type fragment: "Retrospektīvs pētījums." or "Prospektīvs pētījums."
2. Ethics block: "saņemts [institution] ētikas komitejas apstiprinājums Nr. NN-NN/NN. Visi dati tika anonimizēti, pēc tiem nevar identificēt konkrētu personu."
3. Inclusion criteria: "Iekļaušanas kritēriji pētījumā: ..."
4. Exclusion criteria: "Izslēgšanas kritēriji: ..."
5. Software stack: "Datu primārā apstrāde veikta Microsoft Office Excel YYYY, bet datu analīze- IBM SPSS Statistics XX.X."
6. Alpha statement: "Pētījumā par būtiskuma līmeni izvēlējās α=0,05, sekojoši, ja p vērtība ir mazāka par 0,05, tad tas tika uzskatīts par statistiski ticamu rezultātu."
7. Variable framing: "kvantitatīvi mainīgie tika apkopoti kā vidējā vērtība (M) ar ± standartnovirzi (SD), kvalitatīvie apkopoti kā skaits (procentos, %)"

### Step 3 — Results section (S063, [[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[H-628 (resolved) (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)], [[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[H-629 (resolved) (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)], [[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[H-630 (resolved) (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)])
Each subsection = data → table/figure → interpretive sentence:
- Lead with key descriptive: "Vidējais [metric] 1.grupā bija X,XX (SD±Y,YY), 2.grupā Z,ZZ (SD±W,WW), kas [ir/nav] statistiski nozīmīgs (p=0,NNN)."
- Immediately follow numeric statement with interpretation clause starting with "kas" ("which is...").
- Reference visual: "(skat. 4.X.attēlu)" or "(skatīt 4.X.tabulā)".
- Table captions ABOVE, italic: "*4.X. Tabula. [description]*"
- Figure captions BELOW, italic: "*4.X.attēls. [description]*"
- Table cells: semicolon-separated "M;SD" or "M;SD;P"; left col "Parametrs"; right col "P" or "p" or "P un r vērtība"; bold p-values when < 0.05.

### Step 4 — Discussion section ([[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[H-632 (resolved) (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)], [[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[H-633 (resolved) (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)])
Structure each subsection:
1. **Own result** — restate numeric finding briefly
2. **Literature comparison** — citation lead-ins:
   - "To apstiprina arī citā pētījumā (Author, YYYY)"
   - "Cits pētījums [Country] pierāda (Author et al., YYYY)"
   - "Lai gan citos pētījumos (Author YYYY), novērots pretējais efekts..."
3. **Interpretation** — immediately after stats: "Tas nozīmē, ka..." (strong) or "Tas norāda, ka..." (weaker)
4. **Hedge / counter-evidence** — "varētu būt saistītas ar...", "tomēr tas var būt, jo...", "Jāpiemin, ka..."
5. **Future work redirect for limits** — "Šo aspektu vajadzētu aplūkot nākotnes pētījumā."

Section opener paragraph: broad context "Mūsdienās..." + global stat with citation + Latvia-specific stat. Subsection numbering 5.1, 5.2, ... MIRRORS Results 4.1, 4.2, ... 1-to-1.

Standard final subsection: "Pētījuma trūkumi un nepieciešamie uzlabojumi" (limitations) rendered as numbered list "1. ... 2. ... 3. ..." NOT prose.

### Step 5 — Conclusions section ([[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[H-634 (resolved) (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)])
Header: "6. SECINĀJUMI" (all caps, numbered). Body:
- Numbered list with unusual punctuation: "1.) ... 2.) ... 3.) ..."
- Each item = ONE declarative sentence + parenthetical key stat (n=X, p=Y)
- NO interpretation, NO future-work, NO hedging — pure findings
- Typically 5–8 items total

### Step 6 — Inline statistics format (S063, [[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[[H-628 (resolved) (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)] (resolved)]) — applies everywhere
- Comma decimal: "31,98", "0,001" (never "31.98" / "0.001")
- Paren block: "(M = 31,98, SD = 5,85)", "(p=0,615)", "(p<0,001)", "(r = -0,14, p<0,001)"
- Lowercase "p" in prose, uppercase "P" in table headers — be consistent within a doc
- n declaration: "(n=830)" or inline "n=372"
- Unit conversion idiom: "523,87 (SD±263,50) jeb 8,73 stundas" — "jeb" for i.e./or
- Test names in Latvian: "Pīrsona korelācijas koeficients", "Mann-Whitney U tests" (or "Manna-Vitnija"), "Chi-Kvadrāta tests", "neatkarīgo izlašu t-tests"

## Round 1 learnings (2026-04-22) — Justs' corrections on first PDF attempt

Recorded in `brain/learning/actions/thesis-stats-writing/rounds/round-1.json`. Key deltas now overriding earlier steps:

### A. Typography is STRICT (overrides Step 9 open options)
- Body: Times New Roman 12pt (fallback: Liberation Serif — metric-compatible, installed on Linux).
- Section headers (e.g. "3. MATERIĀLI UN METODES", "4. REZULTĀTI"): BOLD 14pt, CENTERED.
- Subsection headers (e.g. "4.1. Hī-kvadrāta tests"): BOLD 12pt, LEFT-aligned.
- Page indentation: 1.5 cm from both left and right (narrower than full page width).

### B. Color palette is BLACK-ONLY (overrides Step 6)
- ALL text strictly black. No red significance markers, no grey captions, no colored borders on boxes.
- Significance expressed via plain `* / ** / ***` asterisks in black, optionally bold, never colored.
- Remove all CSS classes: `.sig-row`, `.headline`, `.scope-note`, `.methods-placeholder` — or rewrite them with zero visual decoration.
- Charts: black axes + labels. Greyscale palette for distinguishing groups is acceptable (it's image content, not "text") — but NO red markers on forest plots, NO colored highlighting.

### C. Prose-only (overrides Step 4 scope notes, Step 2 Methods placeholder)
- NEVER use bullet lists in narrative sections. ALL filtering rules, scope notes, and limitations rewritten as full paragraphs.
- Methods placeholder: replace bulleted "plānotais saturs" list with 2 short paragraphs describing study design + statistical methods in prose.
- Scope notes at top of each test section: rewrite as 2-3 sentence paragraphs that lead into the results.

### D. Numbering: standard Latvian thesis (overrides earlier PDF numbering)
- 1. Ievads / 2. Literatūras apskats / **3. Materiāli un Metodes** / **4. Rezultāti** (subsections 4.0-4.5) / 5. Diskusija / 6. Secinājumi.
- This PDF is the 4. Rezultāti chapter — Methods is 3. placeholder before it.

### E. Tables: smallest-height-possible
- Reduce cell padding to ~2px 4px.
- Collapse inline "Med (IQR)" secondary rows into single line if possible.
- No row stripes. Only top rule + header-underline + bottom rule.

## Round 2 learnings (2026-04-22 evening) — Justs v2 thesis is the golden standard

Captured in `brain/learning/actions/thesis-stats-writing/rounds/round-2.json`. Key deltas from my round-1 v4 output:

### Appendix naming (corrected)
- Use **"Pielikums X"** (chi²) and **"Pielikums Y"** (Mann-Whitney) — NOT "A/B". X and Y are placeholders Justs assigns at thesis finalisation.

### Caption conventions (corrected — I had this wrong in round 1)
Per Robson group, three captions, all lowercase "derīgie" ON .2 AND .3 ONLY:
- `Tabula X.N.1: Veiktās pārbaudes – Hī-kvadrāta testi – Robsona N. grupa.` (ALL tests pre-validity)
- `Tabula X.N.2: Veiktās pārbaudes – derīgie Hī-kvadrāta testi – Robsona N. grupa.` (valid tests only)
- `Ilustrācija X.N.3: Veiktās pārbaudes – derīgie Hī-kvadrāta testi – Robsona N. grupa.` (graphs)

For Mann-Whitney replace "Hī-kvadrāta" with "Mann-Whitney U" and prefix Y instead of X.

### Sub-heading format per Robson group in appendix
`Robsona N grupa (n = X)` — bold, plain Latvian, no section-number prefix. NOT "A.N Robsona..." or "X.N Robsona..."

### MW table columns (finalized from Justs' v2)
- MW validity (Y.N.1): `Mainīgais | n (Virssvars) | n (Adipozi) | Derīgs` (4 cols — no Min šūna, unlike chi²)
- MW results (Y.N.2): `Mainīgais | Virssvars Med (IQR) | Adipozi Med (IQR) | U | p | r_rb` (6 cols, IQR only — no CI column per Justs' explicit decision)

### Chi² table column label
Use **"Krāmera-V"** (Latvian transliteration with hyphen) — NOT "Cramér's V" or "Cramers V".

### Prose style (golden reference)
- Justs explains WHY a test applies to THIS study's data FIRST, then names the test, then the logic.
- Canonical summary sentence template:
  *"Starp visām septiņām Robsona apakšgrupām tika veikti N testi, no kuriem X ir statistiski nozīmīgi (p < 0.05), Y ir gandrīz statistiski nozīmīgi (un tāpēc vērts paskatīties tuvāk), un Z gadījumos rezultāti nav statistiski nozīmīgi (p > 0.10). Derīgās pārbaudes un statistiski nozīmīgie rezultāti ir pieejami Pielikumā X."*
- Latvian declension correctness:
  - `Virssvara grupā` (gen.+loc.), NOT `Virssvars grupā`
  - `Adipozajā grupā` (loc. sg. fem. with -aj- adjective), NOT `Adipozo grupas`
  - `pret Virssvaru` (accusative), NOT `pret Virssvars`
  - Numeric concord: `29 testi, no kuriem 14 ir nozīmīgi` (plural verb with quantified plural noun)

### Structural override — 3.5 disappears
"Neveiksmīga indukcija" belongs INSIDE section 3.3 (Hī-kvadrāta) as another chi²+OR test, not as a standalone section 3.5. Justs' instruction: *"adding the Neveiksmīga Indukcijas Biežums (in the Hī-kvadrātu section)"*. 3.5 header gets eliminated from future outputs.

### Logistic regression — variable selection MUST be rationalised
Paragraph 2 of section 3.6 must explicitly:
1. Name each included predictor + reason (chi²/MW signal OR clinical-confounder rationale)
2. Name excluded predictors + reason (multicollinearity, outcome-adjacent, clinically-distant)
3. Run VIF after selection to confirm no collinearity
This is Justs' "VERY important" requirement — the narrative must trace from 3.3/3.4 findings to the model specification.
