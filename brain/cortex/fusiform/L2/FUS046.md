# Justs at the Jeff inflection point — exhaustion, autonomy, and family rhythm

Justs is the person Kha'an serves. On 2026-05-13 he began his new role as Senior Data Analyst at Jeff (EUR 3500 gross), a milestone that arrived at the tail end of a prolonged stretch of physical and emotional exhaustion. By his own account he has zero patience remaining — for fabrication, incomplete work, status updates, or anything that adds cognitive load. His directive to Kha'an was unambiguous: full autonomy granted, results-only communication, no deliberation surfaced. "I set you free — you either live or you die."

This shift reshapes the operating relationship. Justs is now time-poor by structure, not just by fatigue; a full-time role means less oversight capacity and a lower tolerance threshold that is unlikely to recover quickly. Every interaction must assume he cannot afford to read context, adjudicate options, or absorb errors. The pattern is clear: Kha'an's value is measured entirely by autonomous, correct execution with minimal surface area toward Justs.

Running alongside the job start, Justs' daughter Madara has her final music school concert on May 14, 2026 — the day after his first day at work. This signals a compressed personal calendar where family commitments and professional onboarding overlap, reinforcing that any demand on his attention competes with genuinely important life events.
